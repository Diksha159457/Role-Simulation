## Axis 2 — Orientation: Entitlement ↔ Contribution

**Q [A2_OPEN]:** Think of the most energy-consuming part of your day. How did you show up in it?

**A:** I gave more than was expected of me

> *Tree routes to: A2_Q_CONTRIBUTION*

---

**Q [A2_Q_CONTRIBUTION]:** Was there a moment today you did something for someone else — not because you had to, but because you could?

**A:** Yes — I helped a colleague with something not in my job description

> *Signal recorded: axis2:contribution. Routes to: A2_R_CONTRIBUTION*

---

**💡 Reflection [A2_R_CONTRIBUTION]:**
Giving without being asked is easy to overlook in your own accounting of the day. But it compounds — both in what you build and in who you become. You chose to invest in someone else's day. That's not small.

*🌉 Bridge: "You've looked at what you gave. Now let's look at who you were thinking about."*

## Axis 3 — Radius: Self-Centric ↔ Altrocentric

**Q [A3_OPEN]:** When you think about today's most difficult moment, whose experience was in your mind?

**A:** Someone downstream — the end user or someone we serve

> *Tree routes to: A3_Q_BEYOND (the widest radius branch)*

---

**Q [A3_Q_BEYOND]:** When the person you're ultimately serving came to mind — the user, the customer, the stakeholder — what did that do to your energy?

**A:** It grounded me — reminded me why this matters

> *Signal recorded: axis3:transcendent. Routes to: A3_R_TRANSCENDENT*

---

**💡 Reflection [A3_R_TRANSCENDENT]:**
When the person you're serving enters your frame, the work changes. It's no longer just a task — it's a contribution to something. Maslow called this self-transcendence: the most reliable source of meaning humans have. You touched that today.
