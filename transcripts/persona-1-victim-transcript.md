**Q [A1_Q_HARD]:** You said it felt 'Stormy — things went sideways'. When the day pushed back, what was your first honest instinct?

**Q [A1_Q_EXTERNAL]:** When you felt blocked today, was there any moment — however small — where you still had a choice?

## Axis 2 — Orientation: Entitlement ↔ Contribution

**Q [A2_OPEN]:** Think of the most energy-consuming part of your day. How did you show up in it?

**A:** I felt I wasn't getting enough back for what I gave

> *Tree routes to: A2_Q_ENTITLEMENT*

---

**Q [A2_Q_ENTITLEMENT]:** When you think about what you deserved to receive today — recognition, help, a fair workload — what comes up?

**A:** I expected more acknowledgment than I got

> *Signal recorded: axis2:entitlement*

---

**💡 Reflection [A2_R_ENTITLEMENT]:**
Feeling underrecognised is real data — not a character flaw. But there's a useful question buried in it: when you were looking for what you deserved, were you also looking for what you could give? Both can be true at once. Tomorrow is a new ledger.

> *Critically: the reflection does not shame the entitlement feeling. It reframes it as information and opens a door without pushing.*

---

*🌉 Bridge: "You've looked at what you gave. Now let's look at who you were thinking about."*

---

## Axis 3 — Radius: Self-Centric ↔ Altrocentric

**Q [A3_OPEN]:** When you think about today's most difficult moment, whose experience was in your mind?

**A:** Mine — I was focused on getting through it

> *Tree routes to: A3_Q_SELF*

---

**Q [A3_Q_SELF]:** Staying focused on your own experience isn't wrong — sometimes it's necessary. But was there anyone else affected by what you were going through today?

**A:** Probably yes, but I didn't have bandwidth to notice

> *Signal recorded: axis3:self. Routes to: A3_R_SELF*

---

**💡 Reflection [A3_R_SELF]:**
There's no shame in a day that was all about survival. But here's something worth sitting with: the people around you were in your orbit today too. Bandwidth is real — so is its opposite. Tomorrow, one small act of noticing someone else changes the texture of the day.

## Summary

Today's reflection: You described the day as 'Stormy — things went sideways'. On agency, you leaned external — the day's forces felt loud — but you're here, which means you navigated anyway. On contribution, you showed entitlement — you wanted recognition — which means you cared enough to have standards. On radius, your frame was self — your focus was close-in — sometimes that's exactly right. That's today. Tomorrow is unwritten.
